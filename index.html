C3R: Cross-Modal Cycle Consistency Rewards Improve Multimodal Reasoning

Paper arXiv

C3R addresses the modality gap in multimodal large language models by turning cross-modal contradictions into dense, label-free rewards. Given paired visual (e.g., webpage screenshots) and textual (e.g., HTML) views of the same content, our method enforces a four-way cycle consistency over image and text, teaching the model to resolve its own internal conflicts and improving robustness on web and document understanding tasks.

In many real-world pages, the base multimodal model produces different answers when queried on the screenshot versus on the underlying HTML. C3R identifies such cross-modal disagreements and uses them as hard examples: by rewarding cycles that remain consistent across modalities, the model becomes both more accurate and more self-consistent on challenging benchmarks.

Our four-way cycle constructs latent queries from a candidate answer in both the text and image views, then performs forward inference along T→T, T→I, I→T, and I→I paths. Cycles that reconstruct the original answer consistently receive higher reward, while inconsistent cycles are penalized, effectively aligning multimodal representations without human-labeled QA pairs.

Abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) suffer from a fundamental “modality gap,” contradicting themselves on visual versus text views of the same content. This paper argues that this inconsistency is not a failure, but a powerful resource for self-reward multimodal learning. Instead of relying on flawed voting mechanisms that amplify systematic errors when the majority is wrong, we introduce cross-modal cycle consistency as rewards (C3R) to improve multimodal reasoning. C3R performs backward inference from an answer to a query, switches modalities, and performs forward inference to verify the answer’s consistency. This cycle serves as a dense, label-free reward that guides the model to resolve its own internal conflicts, while avoiding majority-is-wrong failures of standard voting methods. On standard benchmarks, C3R mitigates modality-specific biases and improves reasoning accuracy by up to 7.6 points. Our results show that robust reasoning emerges not just from scaling data, but from achieving a bidirectional understanding of the multimodal world.

Method Overview: Cross-Modal Cycle Consistency Rewards

C3R starts from a candidate answer and performs backward inference to reconstruct latent queries from both the text view xT and the image view xI. Each reconstructed query is then used for forward inference across both modalities, producing four reconstructed answers along the T→T, T→I, I→T, and I→I paths. Cycle consistency is measured by whether these reconstructed answers remain consistent with the original candidate. Consistent cycles receive higher reward, while inconsistent ones are penalized, and this cycle-consistency reward is used in a GRPO objective to improve the multimodal LLM’s reasoning capability without any human-labeled QA pairs.

Inference Overview

Our framework operates on naturally occurring multimodal data, such as webpages with both screenshots and HTML source, or visual question answering benchmarks where we construct complementary text views via captioning. During training, the model samples candidate answers, performs backward inference to obtain synthetic queries, and then executes cross-modal forward passes to reconstruct the answers. Comparing the reconstructed answers with the originals yields a binary cycle-consistency reward, which we plug into a GRPO objective to update the multimodal LLM. At inference time, the trained model exhibits improved accuracy and consistency across both visual and textual inputs on benchmarks including ScienceQA, ChartQA, InfoVQA, MathVista, A-OKVQA, and Visual Web Arena.
